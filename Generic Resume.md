1. Contact Information
This is the "how do I find you" section. Keep it professional and uncluttered.

Full Name: Largest font on the page.

Phone Number: Use a personal cell number.

Professional Email: Something like firstname.lastname@email.com.

LinkedIn Profile: A link to your updated profile.

Location: City and State is sufficient (no need for a full home address anymore).

Portfolio/GitHub: Relevant for creatives or tech roles.

2. Professional Summary or Objective
A brief (2–3 sentence) "elevator pitch" at the top.

Summary: Best if you have experience. Highlights your years in the field and your biggest wins.

Objective: Best for entry-level or career changers. Focuses on what you bring to the table and your career goals.

3. Core Skills
A quick-glance section of your "tools of the trade."

Hard Skills: Specific software (Python, Photoshop), languages, or technical methodologies.

Soft Skills: Use these sparingly; it’s better to show leadership in your experience than just list the word "leadership."

4. Professional Experience
The meat of the resume. List this in reverse-chronological order (most recent job first).

Job Title & Company Name.

Dates of Employment.

Bullet Points: Focus on achievements, not just duties.

Pro Tip: Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Instead of saying "Managed a team," say "Managed a team of 10 to increase sales by 15% in Q3."

5. Education
Keep it concise, especially if you’ve been out of school for a while.

Degree and Major.

University Name and Location.

Graduation Year (Optional if you're worried about ageism, but standard for recent grads).

Honors/GPA: Only include if it’s impressive (usually 3.5 or higher).

6. Optional Sections
If you have space and they add value, consider:

Certifications & Licenses: (e.g., PMP, AWS Certified).

Projects: Great for students or developers to show hands-on work.

Volunteer Work: Shows character and additional skills.

Languages: If you are bilingual or multilingual.

Quick Checklist for Success:
Length: Keep it to 1 page if you have under 10 years of experience; 2 pages max for veterans.

Format: Save it as a PDF to ensure the formatting doesn't break when opened.

Keywords: Tailor your resume for every job application by using words found in the specific job description to pass Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).
